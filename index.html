Daniel's Seventy Weeks - Daniel 9 : 24-27

Uncover the profound prophecy of Daniel's Seventy Weeks, understanding its significance in the timeline of end-time events.

"Seventy weeks are decreed about your people and your holy city, to finish the transgression, to put an end to sin, and to atone for iniquity, to bring in everlasting righteousness, to seal both vision and prophet, and to anoint a most holy place." - Daniel 9:24 (ESV)

Breakdown of The Years

  • The First 7 Weeks (49 years): The rebuilding of Jerusalem and the restoration of the Jewish people after the Babylonian exile.
  • The Next 62 Weeks (434 years): This is the time leading up to the coming of the Messiah, encompassing significant historical events such as the rise and fall of empires and the ministry of Jesus Christ.
  • The Final Week (7 years): This period is linked to the end times, with various events that will unfold, including the rise of the Antichrist, tribulation, and the second coming of Christ.

Detailed Breakdown of Each Segment:

The Jews had been in exile and Daniel understood from Jeremiah’s prophecy that God would bring them back to the Promised Land after seventy years. As Daniel was deep in prayer, the angel, Gabriel, appeared to him. God had sent Gabriel to give Daniel understanding concerning Israel’s destiny beyond those seventy years in exile. It concerned Israel’s future until the end.

Essentially, God has decreed 70 weeks of years, meaning 490 years (70 times 7) for Israel and the city of Jerusalem before the end will come. Bear in mind that these are lunar years (360 days a year) and not solar years (365.25 days a year), which we are more accustomed to. During these 70 weeks of years, God will deal with Israel’s sin and rebellion against His covenant, and He will turn the nation back to Him. At the same time, all the prophecies pertaining to Israel and the present world will be completely fulfilled.

The 70 weeks of years are divided into three segments: the first 7 weeks, the next 62 weeks, and the final week. Each segment has its own significance and events that will unfold, leading up to the ultimate fulfillment of God's plan for Israel and the world.

At the Beginning & End of the First 69 Weeks Daniel 9:25-26 (NASB) - 25"So you are to know and discern that from the issuing of a decree to restore and rebuild Jerusalem until Messiah the Prince there will be seven weeks and sixty-two weeks; it will be built again, with plaza and moat, even in times of distress". 26 "Then after the sixty-two weeks the Messiah will be cut off and have nothing, and the people of the prince who is to come will destroy the city and the sanctuary. And its end will come with a flood; even to the end there will be war; desolations are determined.
Daniel 9:27 (NASB) - 27"And he will make a firm covenant with the many for one week, but in the middle of the week he will put a stop to sacrifice and grain offering; and on the wing of abominations will come one who makes desolate, even until a complete destruction, one that is decreed, is poured out on the one who makes desolate."

The countdown would begin on the day when the decree to rebuild the broken walls of Jerusalem was issued. This event happened 93 years later after Daniel was given the vision. A Persian king, Artaxerxes Longimanus, issued the decree for the Jews to rebuild the broken walls and their beloved city. Based on the date given in Nehemiah 2:1 (the month of Nisan on the twentieth year of Artaxerxes’ reign and assuming it falls on the first day of Nisan in the Jewish calendar),the Royal Observatory in Greenwich, United Kingdom, computed the date to be 14 March 445 B.C. According to the prophecy, the 70 weeks of years are divided into 3 periods of 7 weeks, 62 weeks and the final 1 week. At the end of 7 and 62 weeks, which is essentially 69 weeks, “the Messiah will be cut off and have nothing.” 69 weeks of years equal to 483 years(69 x 7) or 173,880 days (69 x 7 x 360). Using 14 March 445 B.C. as the start date, the date for the end of the 69 weeks of years works out to be 6 April A.D. 32. Fascinatingly, this was the day when Jesus rode into Jerusalem on a donkey and presented Himself to the Jews as their Messiah. However, the political and religious Jewish leaders rejected Jesus as the prophecy has indicated -- “the Messiah will be cut off and have nothing.” They executed Him on a cross. They cut Him down and cut Him off. Barely 40 years later, “the people of the prince who is to come,” that is, the Roman armies under the command of Titus Vespasianus destroyed the temple and Jerusalem. The formidable Roman military force literally came in like a flood. As Jesus had also prophesied, “not one stone here will be left upon another, which will not be torn down” (Matthew 24:2). During the next few decades, the Romans continued to put down guerrilla attempts by small Jewish rebel groups until they banished all the Jews from the land.

After 69 weeks of years, the prophetic clock for Israel stopped ticking. The final one week of Daniel’s 70 weeks was suspended indefinitely. In a sense and prophetically speaking, Israel disappeared from the scene and the church emerged and took centre-stage since then until today. This period is known as the church age. It is also known as the time of the Gentiles. We are living in the church age. The church is a mystery (Ephesians 5:32). The entity of the church was unknown to the Old Testament saints. No rabbis or scholars anticipated the emergence of the church. The church is a new creation (2 Corinthians 5:17). It is a new entity that God created after the death and resurrection of Jesus Christ. It is not a continuation of Israel. The church is a spiritual body (1 Corinthians 12:27). It is not a physical entity. It is made up of believers from all nations, tribes and tongues who have been born again by the Spirit of God. The church is the bride of Christ (Ephesians 5:25-27). It is the beloved of Christ. It is the one for whom Christ died and rose again. The church is the temple of God (1 Corinthians 3:16-17). It is the dwelling place of God on earth. The church is the body of Christ (Colossians 1:18). It is the physical manifestation of Christ on earth.

The final week, or 7 years, is linked to the end times and is associated with various events that will unfold, including the rise of the Antichrist, a period of tribulation, and the second coming of Christ. The church age straddles between verse 26 and verse 27. It will come to an end when God decides to rapture the church. On that day, our Lord Jesus will appear in the sky. There will be an element of surprise to it. it will be sudden and unexpected. Suddenly all the faithful Christians will be snatched up by Jesus and caught up to be with Him, and then brought to heaven (I Corinthians 15:51-53; 1 Thessalonians 4;15-17). When this happens, the last one week (7 years) of Daniel’s 70 weeks of years will kick in. The prophetic clock for Israel, which has stopped for more than 2,000 years, will start ticking again. This is the 7-year tribulation period, which we read in the book of Revelation when God unleashes His wrath and judgement on the inhabitants of the earth. God will finish His dealing with Israel in this last 7 years and then the end will come. It will be a terrible time of severe judgement for Israel. There will be wars and catastrophic disasters in Israel. This is the consequence of their rejection of Jesus as their Messiah some 2,000 years ago. Besides the rapture, another political event will also mark the beginning of the final one week of Daniel’s 70 weeks prophecy. The Anti-Christ will make a covenant with Israel and her enemies guaranteeing her peace and security. However, he will break the covenant and turn against Israel at the 3.5-year mark (“in the middle of the weeks”). He will also set up something sacrilegious, described as “the abomination of desolation”(Matthew 24:15)in the rebuilt Jewish temple. The good news is that finally all the remnants of Israel will be saved (Zechariah 12;10, 13:8-9; Romans 11:26). At the end of 7 years of Tribulation, Christ Jesus will return to the earth with the armies of heaven, which includes the church (Revelation 19:7-8, 14). He will fight and utterly defeat the Anti-Christ (the beast) and the kings of the earth and all their armies that are gathered to fight Him and Israel (Revelation 19:19-21). Notice that the church is not present during the 7-year Tribulation period. It is God’s appointed time to deal with Israel and the city of Jerusalem. Meanwhile, the church, which has been raptured 7 years earlier, will return to the earth with Christ Jesus. The church will return as the Bride of Christ (Revelation 19:7-8, 14, 17-18). God’s Sovereignty This amazing prophecy of Daniel’s 70 weeks points to the existence as well as the sovereignty of God over human affairs. God is in absolute control over the world and everything that is happening. He has sovereignly decreed the times for both Israel and the church, and the Jews and the Gentiles. He alone determines how this present age will end. Jesus is the Lord of history and the future. Praise be to our Lord Jesus Christ!

Prophecy of the Statue - Fierce King

Unveil the prophecy of the Statue from the Book of Daniel, understanding its significance in the context of end-time events.

"And in the latter time of their kingdom, when the transgressors have reached their fullness, a king of fierce countenance, and understanding dark sentences, shall stand up." - Daniel 8:23 (KJV)

Let's break this all down

Explore our detailed breakdowns of these prophecies, providing clarity and understanding of their implications for believers today.

The Statue's Composition :

  • Head of Gold: Represents the Babylonian Empire, known for its wealth and splendor.
  • Chest and Arms of Silver: Symbolizes the Medo-Persian Empire, which succeeded Babylon and was characterized by its dual governance.
  • Belly and Thighs of Bronze: Denotes the Greek Empire, established by Alexander the Great, known for its cultural influence and military prowess.
  • Legs of Iron: Represents the Roman Empire, renowned for its strength, discipline, and extensive reach.
  • Feet partly of Iron and partly of Clay: Symbolizes a divided kingdom, This represents a future confederation of nations (Modern Times) with inherent weaknesses. This is The United Nations.
The Statue is basically a representation of successive world empires leading to a divided kingdom in the end times. Which aligns with the prophetic vision of Daniel. Think of it as a timeline of human history and divine prophecy. We are at the feet the final seconds of the end times.
"You watched while a stone was cut out without hands, which struck the statue on its feet of iron and clay, and broke them in pieces." - Daniel 2:34 (NKJV)

Lets Break Down The Feet(Our Part in the Timeline) :

The iron component of the feet signifies strength and military power. It reflects the continuation of the Roman Empire's influence in a modern context, emphasizing the role of powerful nations in global affairs. You see this in how the United States exerts its military influence worldwide, often acting as a dominant force in international relations. This strength is crucial in maintaining order but also highlights the challenges of uniting diverse nations under a single banner. This is a representation of strong nations within the United Nations that possess significant military capabilities, shaping global policies and actions. Many of these nations prioritize defense and strategic alliances to safeguard their interests. However, this strength can also lead to conflicts and power struggles, reflecting the inherent tensions within such a confederation. Many have tried and failed to unite the world under one government, and this prophecy shows us why. The iron component underscores the complexities of maintaining unity among powerful nations with differing agendas.

The clay component represents fragility and instability. It highlights the inherent weaknesses within this future confederation of nations (United Nations), suggesting that despite their alliances, they will lack cohesion and strength. This fragility can be seen in the challenges faced by international organizations in achieving consensus and effective governance. The diverse interests and priorities of member states often lead to disagreements and inefficiencies. Moreover, the clay symbolizes the potential for these nations to be easily influenced or divided, underscoring the difficulties in maintaining unity and stability in a complex global landscape. This aspect of the prophecy serves as a reminder of the limitations of human efforts to create lasting peace and order without divine intervention. This confederation will ultimately fail to achieve its goals, leading to further turmoil and setting the stage for the rise of the Antichrist, who will exploit these weaknesses by posing as a savior mocking our GOD, LORD and Savior to establish his dominion over the world.

The stone cut without hands represents divine intervention and the establishment of God's kingdom. It signifies that despite the efforts of human nations to create their own order, it is ultimately God's plan that will prevail. This stone symbolizes the coming of Jesus Christ, who will establish an eternal kingdom that cannot be shaken. It serves as a reminder that no matter how powerful or influential human nations may become, they are ultimately subject to God's sovereignty. The prophecy emphasizes the futility of human efforts to achieve lasting peace and order without divine guidance, highlighting the importance of faith and trust in God's plan for the future. It also underscores the hope and assurance that believers have in the ultimate victory of God's kingdom over all earthly powers.

Jesus uses the parable of the fig tree to illustrate the signs of the end times. Just as the budding of the fig tree indicates that summer is near, the signs He describes will indicate that His return is near. This serves as a reminder for believers to be attentive to the signs around them and to remain faithful in their walk with Christ. Jesus states that when the fig tree's branches become tender and leaves appear, summer is near. Likewise, when "all these things" (signs of the end times) are seen, his return is imminent. Jesus noted, "This generation will certainly not pass away until all these things have happened," often interpreted as the generation witnessing these signs. Symbolism of Israel: In biblical imagery, the fig tree often represents the nation of Israel. Some interpretations link the budding tree to the restoration or spiritual state of Israel, though the primary focus is on the signs of the end times. The emphasis is on recognizing the signs and being prepared for Christ's return, rather than on specific geopolitical events.

In Mark 11:12-25 and Matthew 21:18-22, Jesus curses a leafy but fruitless fig tree, which serves as an acted parable or visual metaphor for spiritual barrenness.

This act symbolized judgment on Israel's religious leaders, who appeared righteous outwardly ("all leaves") but lacked true spiritual fruit.

It serves as a warning against having a superficial religious profession without genuine faith and, for believers, a call to be fruitful in their spiritual lives.

Key Takeaways

  • The importance of bearing spiritual fruit in the lives of believers, demonstrating genuine faith and obedience to God.
  • The need for self-examination among believers to ensure that their faith is not merely outward but is producing the fruits of the Spirit in their lives.
  • The call to repentance and spiritual renewal, recognizing that true faith must be accompanied by a transformed life that reflects the character of Christ.
  • The assurance that God's judgment is just and that those who are spiritually barren will face consequences, while those who bear fruit will be rewarded.
  • The encouragement for believers to cultivate a deep and authentic relationship with Christ, allowing His Spirit to work in their lives to produce lasting spiritual fruit.
  • The reminder that the signs of the end times are not meant to cause fear but to encourage believers to remain steadfast in their faith and to live with an eternal perspective.
  • Prophetic Fulfillment: The parable emphasizes that God's word and prophecies will not fail, and His promises will be fulfilled.

Delve into the biblical accounts of the Nephlim, examing their origins, characteristics, and the implications of their existence for understanding the spiritual and physical realities of the ancient world. This study also explores the various interpretations and theories surrounding the Nephilim, and their significance in the broader context of biblical history and theology.

"The Nephilim were on the earth in those days-and also afterward-when the sons of God went to the daughters of man and had childeren by them. They were the heros of old and men of renown." (Genesis 6:4)


The Nephilim are a fascinating and enigmatic group of beings mentioned in the Bible, specifically in the book of Genesis. They are described as the offspring of the "sons of God" and the "daughters of man" and are associated with giants and heroes of old. The exact nature and identity of the Nephilim have been the subject of much debate and speculation among scholars and theologians, with various interpretations and theories proposed to explain their origins and significance. Some view them as fallen angels or demigods, while others see them as simply human beings who were exceptionally tall or powerful due to their lineage. Regardless of the debate the truth is that the Nephilim were the offspring of the fallen angels and human women, and they were giants who lived in the ancient world. They were known for their great strength and size, and they were considered to be heroes of old and men of renown. The Nephilim were a significant part of the pre-flood world, and their exsistence is often seen as a contributing factor to the wickedness and corruption that led to the flood. Pre Tribulation Rapture vs Post Tribulation Rapture

The Pre-Tribulation Rapture is the belief that believers in Christ will be caught up to meet the Lord in the air before the period of great tribulation begins on earth. This view emphasizes that the Rapture will occur before the outpouring of God's judgment and the rise of the Antichrist, allowing believers to be spared from the intense suffering and persecution that will characterize the tribulation period. Proponents of this view often point to passages such as 1 Thessalonians 4:16-17 and Revelation 3:10 as evidence for a pre-tribulation Rapture, highlighting the promise of deliverance for those who remain faithful to Christ. The Post-Tribulation Rapture is the belief that believers in Christ will be caught up to meet the Lord in the air at the end of the period of great tribulation on earth. This view emphasizes that the Rapture will occur after the outpouring of God's judgment and the rise of the Antichrist, allowing believers to endure through the trials and persecution of the tribulation period before being delivered by Christ at His return. Proponents of this view often point to passages such as Matthew 24:29-31 and Revelation 20:4-6 as evidence for a post-tribulation Rapture.
